1st Place

Medusa and Parkinson's

by Isis Bos

Méduse, ton offrande me laisse aujourd'hui hagard.
Ce soir, où tu as daigné détourner le regard,
A laissé pour seul tableau de ton acte indulgent,
Un corps peu pétrifié, trop trémoussant.

Désormais, lorsque je m'agrippe à mon cadre flou,
M'engouffrant dans ma peinture que je dissous,
J'épie Adam et Eve, qui, même fuyant du paradis,
Entreprennent une fugue statique, où nul ne frémis.

2nd Place

The Mirror Will Always Remember

by Filippa Acosta

I have watched you for decades.
Not like a stranger,
but like the one thing in your home
that never learned to lie.
You used to meet me in the mornings
with rolled up sleeves, wet hair,
a razor in your right hand,
steady as your breath.
You'd smile like you had secrets
you weren't quite ready to tell the day yet.
Now,
you lean in a bit too close,
as if I can find the version of you
that your mind keeps misplacing.
Your eyes flicker,
not because you don't care,
but because blinking now
requires effort
you never used to notice.
Your mouth opens,
searching for a sentence.
I hold the silence with you.

Your hand rises,
wants to trace your cheek,
but halfway there, it forgets the plan.
So it floats,
loyal, shaking.
I've seen this dance many times before.

Do you remember
how you once braided your daughter's hair
when she asked for your magical touch?
How you wrote in cursive,
beautiful love notes in the margins?
You used to be
a man of movement.
Now the world waits
for you to catch up.
But I don't rush you.
I never have.
I hold you still,
even when you forget to look.

Yesterday,
you asked if you were changing.
I wanted to crack.
But I said nothing.
Because I am a mirror.
And mirrors do not speak.
Only keep.

And tonight,
as you pass me one last time,
your reflection flickers,
not clear,
but soft and blurred.
The light trembles in your pupils.
And I think,
Wow, you are still beautiful.
Even like this.
Especially like this.
Even when your hands forget the music,
you still reach for the piano.
Even when your voice forgets the words,
you still hum to your love, anyway.
Even when the world forgets you,
you are still trying to remember.

I will be the last place
you are whole.
And when you are gone,
I will still be here,
a testament,
holding an outline
of the man
you never stopped being.

3rd Place

Untitled

by Chiara Leprieur

In the stillness of the night, I see
A tremor that shakes you next to me.
Your hands that once held steady and strong,
Now tremble, but I still love you all along.

Parkinson may try to steal your grace,
But I'll never let it take your place.
